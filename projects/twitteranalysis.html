Watricity: A Home-Utility Monitoring System

  • Tech Stack: HTML, CSS, JS, Swift, Adobe Creative Cloud, EasyEDA
  • Paper URL: Project Link

Watricity is a network of sensors and computer server systems programmed to notify users of their water and electrical utility usage and deliver feedback to optimize the efficiency of their usage to limit ecological effects and financial costs. In the present-day market for smart home applications, products tend to require extended periods of time to install as well as only offering users broad data about their homes. Watricity on the other hand, provides users real-time data about specific appliances in minimal time. The Watricity smart application interacts seamlessly with home utility usage and EnviroAid’s servers to present real-time data to the user. The energy tracker device recognizes electrical signals passed in an electrical panel to analyze the usage of specific appliances, whereas the water tracker detects the usage of fluid in a home and analyzes those periods of usage. Both trackers then interface with Watricity servers through the app to provide live information to a user. In addition, the Watricity algorithm will take advantage of local weather data to further refine a user’s water and energy utility usage, such as suggesting to close windows to maintain a suitable temperature for the water pipes or advising the user of the precise temperature to set the thermostat to conserve the most energy.
